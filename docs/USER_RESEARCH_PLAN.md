# User Research Plan

## Purpose

Validate that the product solves a real international-student job-search problem and that the labels are understandable without explanation.

## Participants

Recruit at least 5 international students or recent graduates in the UK.

Prioritise:

- Graduate Route holders;
- final-year students planning UK applications;
- students in business, economics, management, psychology, marketing, policy, finance-adjacent, or social-science fields;
- users who are actively applying.

## Interview Questions

Ask:

1. What is your current visa situation?
2. When does your current visa expire?
3. What types of roles are you applying for?
4. Which job boards or platforms do you check?
5. How do you currently decide whether a role is worth applying to?
6. Which visa/right-to-work phrases confuse you?
7. Have you applied to roles and later discovered visa issues?
8. What would make you trust a visa-related label?
9. What would make you distrust it?
10. Can you show 3 job ads you were unsure about?

## Usability Test Script

Give the user the prototype and say:

> Use this as if you were deciding what to apply to today. I am testing the product, not you.

Tasks:

1. Set your visa profile.
2. Find a role you would apply to.
3. Find a role you would verify first.
4. Open the evidence panel and explain what it means.
5. Paste a job description from another site.
6. Save one job and add a note.
7. Export your tracking data.

Do not explain labels unless the user gets stuck. Record where they hesitate.

## What to Capture

For each participant:

- visa situation;
- target roles;
- current workflow;
- biggest pain point;
- confusing phrases;
- jobs they shared;
- whether labels made sense;
- whether evidence was trusted;
- feature requests;
- direct quotes if permission is given.

## Red Flags

The product needs revision if:

- users interpret Worth applying as a guarantee;
- users ignore Verify first;
- users do not understand sponsor-register match limitations;
- evidence phrases are too hidden;
- users mainly want CV/cover-letter tools instead;
- users say freshness/coverage matters more than visa signals.

## Portfolio Use

Summarise research in the README:

- number of participants;
- repeated pain points;
- how research changed the product;
- examples of confusing phrases;
- before/after prototype changes.

This helps show that the project came from a real problem, not a feature idea.
